Master's thesis (2014/15)

In my Master's thesis I did developed a computer vision solution which allowed the user to interact with his hands in the virtual world. The work is based on the Oculus Rift DK1/DK2 and a pair of consumer webcams. The core questions of the thesis are, what requirements are necessary to implement a hand-interaction system for a virtual environment. As using cheap webcams is very challenging, I also used the Leap Motion as a baseline. Therefore the algorithms were tested against the Leap Motion detection and against my own algorithms on the Leap Motion images.

AudEye (2013)

The AudEye project is a software that assists the user while playing chess. We constructed a system in which this player wears eye-tracking glasses (ETG) and an ear-piece. The ETG record both his eye movements and the chess scene itself. This input is then processed, including image recognition to obtain the state of the game. The player communicates with the system by executing eye gestures. In this way he can control the system and request hints for good moves which are calculated by a chess engine. He may also choose to be informed about the usefulness of executing a move with the chess piece he looks at. These values are made audible by the means of sonification. Additionally, a player-vs-computer mode was implemented, the game's protocol is saved and several statistics are collected.

Robocup (2012)

As a team-member of the Team ToBi I participated the Robocup competition in the Robocup@home league which was held in Mexcico City. The task of our team was to improve the robots capabilities on the one hand, where i worked on the Object Detection, and on the tasks the robot has to perform during the competition on the other hand. Therefore we learned a lot about the complexity of a robotic system and also gained insight into the single subproblems like object detection or speech recognition. We were able to reach the finals (Video shows finals demo) and achieved a 3rd place out of ca. 20 Teams.
